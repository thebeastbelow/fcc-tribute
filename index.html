  • 1946 Donald Trump is born in Queens, New York.
  • 1959 Leaves for the New York Military Academy.
  • 1966 Transfers to Penn's Wharton School of Business, and almost immediately begins buying properties in Philadelphia.
  • 1973 The U.S. Justice Department sues the Trump Organization, charging that it frequently violated the Fair Housing Act by discriminating against black potential buyers.
  • 1977 Marries Ivana Zelnickova Winklmayr.
  • 1977 Donald John Trump Jr. is born.
  • 1981 Ivanka Marie Trump is born.
  • 1982 With Mayor Ed Koch, celebrates the completion of the Trump Tower in Manhattan.
  • 1984 Eric Frederic Trump is born.
  • 1987 Publishes The Art of The Deal, which spends 51 weeks on the New York Times bestseller list.
  • 1989 First appears on the cover of Time.
  • 1990 Is removed from the Forbes 400 list, not to be reinstated until 1996.
  • 1990 Separates from Ivana, eventually divorcing in 1992.
  • 1991 Files for bankruptcy for the first time.
  • 1993 Marla Maples gives birth to Tiffany Trump.
  • 1993 Marries Marla.
  • 1996 Buys the Miss Universe Organization.
  • 1999 Divorces Marla.
  • 2000 The Simpsons airs "Bart to the Future," an episode in which Trump becomes president of the United States.
  • 2003 Files suit against Native American tribe and rival developer the Eastern Pequots.
  • 2004 The Apprentice premiers.
  • 2005 Marries Melania Knauss.
  • 2005 Opens Trump University, which is eventually shut down amid lawsuits from students and the New York attorney general alleging it was a scam.
  • 2006 Barron Trump is born.
  • 2007 Receives a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 2012 Offers Barack Obama $5 million to the charity of his choice in exchange for the president releasing his birth certificate.
  • 2014 Sues Trump Entertainment Resorts to remove his name from the Trump Taj Mahal and Trump Plaza casinos, saying they were not up to his standards.
  • 2015 Announces his bid for the U.S. presidency in a speech that included remarks about Mexican immigrants being "rapists" and drug dealers.
  • 2015 NBC cuts business ties with Trump following his immigration remarks.
